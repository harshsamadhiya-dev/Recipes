Masala Paneer

Description

Paneer, a fresh and versatile soft cheese, is a staple ingredient in Indian Cuisine. Adding spices and herbs while making the paneer transforms it from a simple, plain cheese into a flavorful ingredient that can elevate any dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 cup sliced onion
  • 1 inch peeled and sliced ginger
  • 5-6 garlic cloves
  • 2 cups sliced tomatoes
  • 15 whole cashew nuts
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander(dhaniya) powder
  • 250 to 300 grams paneer
  • 4 cloves(laung)
  • 1 to 2 tablespoon coriander leaves(finely chopped)

Steps

  1. To a pan, add 1 tablespoon oil, cardamoms, onions, ginger and garlic. Saute for 4 to 5 mins, until the onions turn transparent. (If you are using ginger garlic paste, saute the onions first for 4 mins and then add the ginger garlic paste to saute for a minute or 2)
  2. Add sliced tomatoes, 1 teaspoon sea salt and cashew nuts.
  3. Stir well and cover the pan. Cook until the tomatoes turn mushy and break down completely. It takes about 7 to 8 minutes on a medium heat. If your pan is dry, add a splash of water, not a lot. Later stir in the red chili powder, coriander powder and garam masala.
  4. Saute on a medium heat for 3 to 4 mins, until aromatic. Turn off and cool down.
  5. Pour 1 cup cold water to a blender jar & transfer the onion tomato masala.
  6. Blend/grind to a very smooth puree. Taste test to see if you need to increase the heat level.
  7. At this stage, if you want you may wipe clean the pan with wet kitchen papers but not essential unless you are using a cast iron.
  8. In the same pan, pour mustard oil on a low heat and add the optional whole spices if using bay leaf, cinnamon and cloves. Let the spices sizzle for 30 seconds and stir in 1/4 teaspoon more chili powder (if you want to adjust the heat level or color).
  9. Add the onion tomato puree to a strainer, held over the pan. Use a spoon to pass the puree.
  10. Pour 1/2 cup more water & mix well. Bring to a gentle boil on a medium heat. Cover and cook until thick, about 10 to 12 mins. Keep stirring every 3 to 4 mins, to prevent burning at the bottom. (The gravy is thick and splatters a lot if you cook covered.)
  11. Add more salt and garam masala at this stage. (taste test before adding more garam masala)
  12. Stir in the cream.
  13. Stir the paneer and cover the pan. Cook on a low heat for 2 to 3 mins until heated through. Turn off.
  14. Garnish with a tablespoon of coriander leaves.
